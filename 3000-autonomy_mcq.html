Autonomy in Team Motivation - MCQ Test

Test your understanding of autonomy as a technique to enhance team motivation with these real-world scenario-based questions.

1. A software development team is working on a new project. The manager assigns tasks but allows team members to choose their preferred programming tools and methodologies. How does this approach impact team motivation?

Correct Answer: b) It increases motivation by fostering ownership and creativity.
Allowing team members to choose their tools and methodologies gives them autonomy, which enhances their sense of ownership and encourages creative problem-solving. This aligns with autonomy as a key motivator in self-determination theory, boosting engagement and productivity.

2. In a marketing team, employees are given the freedom to design their own campaign strategies but must meet specific performance goals. What is the primary benefit of this autonomy?

Correct Answer: b) It encourages innovative and tailored solutions.
Autonomy in designing campaign strategies allows team members to leverage their unique insights and creativity, leading to innovative and effective campaigns. This freedom within defined goals fosters motivation by giving employees control over their work process.

3. A manager notices that team morale is low due to micromanagement. What autonomy-based strategy could improve motivation?

Correct Answer: b) Allow team members to set their own deadlines within project constraints.
Micromanagement often reduces motivation by limiting autonomy. Allowing team members to set their own deadlines within constraints empowers them, increases accountability, and boosts morale by trusting their judgment, aligning with autonomy's role in motivation.

4. A sales team is allowed to decide how to approach clients but must follow a strict script during pitches. How does this balance of autonomy affect motivation?

Correct Answer: b) It may limit motivation due to restricted creativity in pitches.
While autonomy in approaching clients is motivating, a strict script during pitches limits creative expression, which can reduce motivation. Autonomy is most effective when employees have meaningful control over key aspects of their work.

5. A project team is struggling with low engagement. The manager decides to let the team choose their project roles based on their strengths. What is the likely outcome?

Correct Answer: b) Improved engagement due to alignment with personal strengths.
Allowing team members to choose roles based on their strengths provides autonomy and aligns tasks with their interests and skills. This increases intrinsic motivation, as employees feel valued and capable, leading to higher engagement.

6. In a customer service team, employees are given autonomy to resolve customer issues without needing supervisor approval for decisions under $100. How does this impact motivation?

Correct Answer: b) It increases motivation by empowering employees.
Granting autonomy to resolve issues empowers employees, making them feel trusted and capable. This aligns with autonomy as a motivator in self-determination theory, enhancing job satisfaction and engagement.

7. A design team is allowed to experiment with new tools but must adhere to strict brand guidelines. How does this affect their motivation?

Correct Answer: b) It balances motivation by combining freedom with structure.
Autonomy in tool choice fosters creativity and motivation, while brand guidelines provide necessary structure to ensure alignment with organizational goals. This balance supports motivation by offering freedom within clear boundaries.

8. A team is demotivated due to rigid schedules. The manager introduces flexible work hours while maintaining project deadlines. What is the likely impact?

Correct Answer: a) Increased motivation due to greater control over work-life balance.
Flexible work hours provide autonomy over scheduling, allowing employees to manage their work-life balance. This autonomy increases motivation by showing trust in employees' ability to meet deadlines while accommodating personal needs.

9. A manager gives a team autonomy to decide how to allocate a project budget but requires regular progress reports. How does this approach influence motivation?

Correct Answer: b) It enhances motivation by combining autonomy with accountability.
Autonomy in budget allocation empowers the team, while progress reports ensure accountability. This balance fosters motivation by giving employees control over resources while maintaining alignment with project goals.

10. A team with high autonomy reports feeling overwhelmed by decision-making responsibilities. What can the manager do to maintain motivation?

Correct Answer: b) Provide clearer guidelines and support while preserving autonomy.
Too much autonomy without support can lead to overwhelm. Providing guidelines and resources helps employees make decisions confidently while preserving autonomy, maintaining motivation by balancing freedom with structure.
