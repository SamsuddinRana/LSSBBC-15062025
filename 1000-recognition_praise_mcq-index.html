Recognition and Praise Theory MCQ

1. Sarah, a team leader, notices that her team member, John, consistently meets project deadlines. How can she apply Recognition and Praise Theory to motivate John?

  • a) Assign him more tasks without acknowledgment
  • b) Publicly praise John during a team meeting
  • c) Ignore his efforts to avoid favoritism
  • d) Criticize him to push for improvement

Correct Answer: b) Publicly praise John during a team meeting

Recognition and Praise Theory emphasizes acknowledging employees' efforts to boost motivation. Public praise reinforces positive behavior, enhances John's sense of achievement, and encourages continued performance.

2. A manager wants to use Recognition and Praise to improve team morale. Which action aligns best with this theory?

  • a) Offer a monetary bonus only
  • b) Send a personalized thank-you email to each team member
  • c) Increase workload to show trust
  • d) Hold a meeting to discuss errors

Correct Answer: b) Send a personalized thank-you email to each team member

Personalized recognition, like a thank-you email, directly acknowledges individual contributions, fostering a sense of value and boosting morale, a core principle of Recognition and Praise Theory.

3. Why is timely recognition important in the workplace according to Recognition and Praise Theory?

  • a) It reduces the need for salary increases
  • b) It reinforces positive behavior immediately
  • c) It simplifies performance reviews
  • d) It reduces team conflicts

Correct Answer: b) It reinforces positive behavior immediately

Timely recognition strengthens the connection between the action and the reward, making employees feel valued and motivated to repeat the behavior, as per the theory.

4. A team member, Lisa, feels demotivated despite working hard. How can Recognition and Praise Theory help?

  • a) Give her a warning to improve
  • b) Acknowledge her efforts in front of peers
  • c) Assign her a new project
  • d) Reduce her responsibilities

Correct Answer: b) Acknowledge her efforts in front of peers

Public acknowledgment of Lisa’s hard work validates her efforts, increases her sense of belonging, and boosts motivation, aligning with the theory’s focus on recognition.

5. What is a potential downside of inconsistent recognition in a team?

  • a) Increased productivity
  • b) Feelings of unfairness or demotivation
  • c) Reduced need for feedback
  • d) Improved team collaboration

Correct Answer: b) Feelings of unfairness or demotivation

Inconsistent recognition can make employees feel overlooked or undervalued, leading to demotivation and perceptions of unfairness, which undermines the theory’s effectiveness.

6. How does Recognition and Praise Theory differ from monetary rewards in motivating teams?

  • a) It focuses on financial incentives
  • b) It emphasizes emotional and social validation
  • c) It requires no manager involvement
  • d) It prioritizes task delegation

Correct Answer: b) It emphasizes emotional and social validation

Unlike monetary rewards, Recognition and Praise Theory focuses on non-financial acknowledgment, such as verbal praise or public recognition, to fulfill emotional and social needs, enhancing motivation.

7. A company implements a monthly 'Employee of the Month' program. How does this align with Recognition and Praise Theory?

  • a) It reduces the need for daily feedback
  • b) It publicly acknowledges outstanding performance
  • c) It focuses on financial rewards
  • d) It simplifies task assignments

Correct Answer: b) It publicly acknowledges outstanding performance

The 'Employee of the Month' program publicly recognizes exceptional contributions, aligning with the theory’s emphasis on visible praise to motivate employees.

8. Why might private recognition be preferred over public recognition for some employees?

  • a) It is less effective in motivating
  • b) Some employees feel uncomfortable with public attention
  • c) It requires less effort from managers
  • d) It avoids the need for feedback

Correct Answer: b) Some employees feel uncomfortable with public attention

Recognition and Praise Theory suggests tailoring recognition to individual preferences. Private recognition respects employees who may feel shy or embarrassed by public praise, ensuring motivation.

9. A manager praises a team for completing a project but doesn’t specify contributions. What’s the impact?

  • a) It fully motivates all team members
  • b) It may reduce motivation due to lack of specificity
  • c) It eliminates the need for individual feedback
  • d) It simplifies team management

Correct Answer: b) It may reduce motivation due to lack of specificity

Specific recognition, per the theory, is more effective as it clearly links praise to individual contributions, making employees feel valued. Vague praise may seem insincere and less motivating.

10. How can Recognition and Praise Theory improve team collaboration?

  • a) By reducing the need for teamwork
  • b) By recognizing collective achievements
  • c) By focusing only on individual performance
  • d) By eliminating feedback sessions

Correct Answer: b) By recognizing collective achievements

Recognizing team efforts fosters a sense of unity and shared purpose, encouraging collaboration and reinforcing positive group dynamics, as supported by the theory.
