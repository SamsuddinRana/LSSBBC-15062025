Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory MCQ

1. At a software company, Sarah feels unmotivated because her salary is below industry standards. According to Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory, this is an example of a:

Answer: b) Hygiene factor

Hygiene factors, such as salary, company policies, and working conditions, do not motivate when present but cause dissatisfaction when absent or inadequate. Sarah’s low salary is a hygiene factor causing dissatisfaction.

2. John is excited about his new project because it allows him to solve complex problems creatively. This aligns with which aspect of Herzberg’s theory?

Answer: b) Motivator factor

Motivator factors, like challenging work, achievement, and recognition, lead to job satisfaction. John’s excitement about creative problem-solving is a motivator factor.

3. A manager notices that employees are demotivated due to poor office lighting and cramped workspaces. These are examples of:

Answer: b) Hygiene factors

Working conditions, such as lighting and workspace, are hygiene factors. Their inadequacy causes dissatisfaction, but improving them alone won’t necessarily motivate employees.

4. Emma feels fulfilled because her manager publicly praised her for leading a successful project. This is an example of a:

Answer: b) Motivator factor

Recognition for achievement is a motivator factor, as it directly contributes to job satisfaction and fulfillment, as per Herzberg’s theory.

5. A company introduces flexible working hours to reduce employee turnover. According to Herzberg, this addresses:

Answer: b) Hygiene factors

Flexible working hours are part of company policies, a hygiene factor. Improving them can reduce dissatisfaction but won’t necessarily lead to high motivation.

6. Tom is unmotivated because his role offers no opportunities for career advancement. This is related to which factor in Herzberg’s theory?

Answer: b) Motivator factor

Opportunities for advancement and growth are motivator factors. Their absence leads to a lack of job satisfaction, as seen in Tom’s case.

7. A team feels demotivated because their manager micromanages their work. This is an example of a poor:

Answer: b) Hygiene factor

Interpersonal relationships with supervisors, like micromanagement, are hygiene factors. Poor relationships cause dissatisfaction but don’t directly motivate when improved.

8. Lisa loves her job because she has autonomy to make decisions. This reflects which factor in Herzberg’s theory?

Answer: b) Motivator factor

Autonomy and responsibility are motivator factors, as they contribute to job satisfaction by making the work itself more fulfilling, as in Lisa’s case.

9. A company provides health insurance to all employees, but motivation levels remain low. Why might this be, according to Herzberg?

Answer: b) Health insurance is a hygiene factor

Health insurance is a hygiene factor. Providing it prevents dissatisfaction, but it doesn’t inherently motivate employees, as motivation comes from motivator factors like achievement or recognition.

10. To boost team motivation, a manager assigns more challenging tasks to employees. This strategy focuses on improving:

Answer: b) Motivator factors

Challenging tasks enhance motivator factors like achievement and responsibility, which directly increase job satisfaction and motivation, according to Herzberg’s theory.
