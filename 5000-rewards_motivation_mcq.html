Rewards Technique for Team Motivation - MCQ

Question 1: In a software development team, the manager notices that developers are working overtime to meet deadlines but seem disengaged. To boost motivation, the manager decides to implement a reward system. Which approach is most likely to enhance intrinsic motivation?

Correct Answer: b) Provide public recognition for creative solutions during team meetings.

Explanation: Intrinsic motivation stems from internal satisfaction, such as feeling valued or accomplished. Public recognition for creative solutions acknowledges individual contributions and fosters a sense of purpose, enhancing intrinsic motivation. Options a), c), and d) are extrinsic rewards (monetary or tangible), which may boost short-term performance but are less effective for sustaining long-term engagement.

Question 2: A sales team exceeds its quarterly targets, and the manager wants to reward them. However, the budget is limited. What is the most cost-effective reward that still motivates the team?

Correct Answer: b) A personalized thank-you note from the CEO.

Explanation: Non-monetary rewards like personalized thank-you notes are highly cost-effective and can significantly boost morale by making employees feel appreciated. Options a), c), and d) involve higher costs and may not be as impactful for long-term motivation, especially if the team values recognition over material rewards.

Question 3: A marketing team is launching a new campaign, and the manager wants to use rewards to encourage collaboration. Which reward structure would best promote teamwork?

Correct Answer: b) Offer a team-based reward, like a group outing, if the campaign succeeds.

Explanation: Team-based rewards encourage collaboration by aligning individual efforts toward a collective goal. A group outing fosters camaraderie and reinforces teamwork. Options a), c), and d) focus on individual contributions, which may create competition and undermine collaborative efforts.

Question 4: A project team feels demotivated due to delayed rewards for their last successful project. What is the best way to address this issue in the future?

Correct Answer: b) Provide immediate recognition after project milestones.

Explanation: Timely rewards reinforce positive behavior and maintain motivation. Immediate recognition after milestones keeps the team engaged throughout the project. Option a) may raise expectations without addressing timing, c) doesn’t solve the delay issue, and d) further delays rewards, worsening the problem.

Question 5: An employee in a customer service team prefers flexible work hours over monetary rewards. How should the manager incorporate this preference into the reward system?

Correct Answer: b) Offer flexible work hours as a reward for excellent performance.

Explanation: Personalizing rewards to match individual preferences increases their effectiveness. Offering flexible work hours aligns with the employee’s needs, boosting motivation. Options a), c), and d) disregard the employee’s preference, reducing the reward’s impact.

Question 6: A manager rewards only the top performers in a team, causing resentment among others. How can the manager improve the reward system to ensure fairness?

Correct Answer: b) Recognize contributions from all team members based on their roles.

Explanation: Fairness in rewards involves recognizing diverse contributions, not just top performers. Acknowledging each member’s role fosters inclusivity and reduces resentment. Option a) perpetuates the issue, c) demotivates the team, and d) undermines performance-driven motivation.

Question 7: A team is working on a long-term project with no immediate results. What type of reward would keep the team motivated throughout?

Correct Answer: b) Small, frequent rewards for achieving interim milestones.

Explanation: Long-term projects require sustained motivation, which small, frequent rewards for milestones provide by breaking the project into manageable goals. Options a), c), and d) delay rewards, risking disengagement during the project.

Question 8: A manager uses only extrinsic rewards (e.g., bonuses) to motivate a design team, but creativity declines. Why might this be happening?

Correct Answer: b) Extrinsic rewards can undermine intrinsic motivation for creative tasks.

Explanation: Over-reliance on extrinsic rewards can reduce intrinsic motivation for tasks requiring creativity, as employees may focus on the reward rather than the process. This is known as the “overjustification effect.” Options a), c), and d) don’t directly address the link between extrinsic rewards and creativity.

Question 9: A manager wants to align rewards with organizational goals. The company prioritizes customer satisfaction. What reward system would best support this goal?

Correct Answer: b) Recognize employees who receive positive customer feedback.

Explanation: Aligning rewards with organizational goals means incentivizing behaviors that support those goals. Recognizing positive customer feedback directly ties rewards to customer satisfaction. Options a), c), and d) focus on unrelated metrics, which may not improve customer experience.

Question 10: A team feels that rewards are distributed inconsistently, leading to distrust. How can the manager rebuild trust in the reward system?

Correct Answer: c) Establish clear, transparent criteria for earning rewards.

Explanation: Transparency in reward systems builds trust by ensuring everyone understands how rewards are earned. Clear criteria reduce perceptions of favoritism or unfairness. Options a) and b) obscure the process, while d) doesn’t address the root cause of distrust.
